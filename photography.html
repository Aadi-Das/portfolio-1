Depth of Field

The next task had to do with the depth of field. And for this, you had to play around with the aperture settings. So the wider the aperture is the foreground will be more in focus, while the background will be more blurred and out of focus, kind of like portrait mode on iphone. While, with a narrow aperture, most of the image will be sharper, including the background. I again had to use a table for this. Aperture from bigger to smaller. You can especially see this with the light in the background getting more in focus


Noise

For this next task, we had to play with the iso setting to see what that does in terms of noise or grain for each of the pictures. I could see the differences more when I took pictures of my candle in the dim room. A higher iso creates more noise or grain, while being brighter, even around the candle. The lower ios is darker around the candle, but has less grain or noise. So a higher iso can be used when you want to make something look more vintage. I had to use my table for this. High to low iso.
